# Helm Business Terms (Proposed)

## 1. Overview

Helm Business is intended for:

- organizations
- managed environments
- compliance-driven use cases

It provides advanced capabilities beyond Free and Pro editions.

---

## 2. License Scope

An enterprise license may grant:

- Use across multiple devices and users
- Centralized policy enforcement
- Access to enterprise features
- Priority support

Licensing may be based on:

- number of users
- number of devices
- organizational size

---

## 3. Enterprise Features

Potential enterprise features include:

### Policy Enforcement

- Package and toolchain restrictions
- Approved software lists
- Version pinning policies

### Drift Management

- Detection of unauthorized changes
- Compliance reporting
- Automated remediation

### Audit & Logging

- Centralized logs
- Compliance exports
- Audit trails

### Managed Environments

- Pre-configured device states
- Bootstrap configurations
- Offline policy enforcement

### Access Control

- Role-based permissions
- Administrative controls

---

## 4. Deployment Models

Helm Business may support:

- local-only deployments
- self-hosted control planes
- hybrid models

Cloud-hosted services may be offered separately.

---

## 5. Support and SLAs

Enterprise licenses may include:

- priority support
- response time guarantees
- onboarding assistance

---

## 6. Restrictions

Enterprise customers may NOT:

- redistribute Helm externally
- resell Helm as a competing product
- bypass licensing controls

---

## 7. Compliance

Helm Business may provide tools to assist with:

- internal compliance requirements
- audit readiness
- reporting

However, compliance responsibility remains with the customer.

---

## 8. Data Handling

Helm is designed to operate locally.

Any data collection or telemetry will be:

- transparent
- configurable
- minimal

Enterprise customers may have additional controls.

---

## 9. Security

Helm Business may include:

- signed updates
- policy verification
- secure execution boundaries

---

## 10. Termination

Enterprise licenses may be:

- subscription-based
- time-limited

Upon expiration:

- enterprise features may be disabled
- core functionality may remain available

## 11. Custom Agreements

Enterprise customers may enter into:

- custom licensing agreements
- negotiated terms

## 12. Future Evolution

Enterprise features and terms will evolve over time.
