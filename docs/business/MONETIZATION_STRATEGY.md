# Helm Monetization Strategy

⚠️ Non-binding document.

Defines Helm’s monetization approach across:

- Pre-1.0 (beta)
- Post-1.0 (commercial release)

---

## 1. Goals

- Sustain long-term development
- Preserve user trust
- Maintain adoption
- Avoid vendor lock-in

---

## 2. Pre-1.0 (Beta Phase)

Helm is currently in beta.

### Strategy

- All features are available
- Pro features are labeled but not gated
- Monetization is optional

---

### 2.1 Support Model

Users may support Helm via:

- GitHub Sponsors
- Donations
- Early access programs

---

### 2.2 Suggested Tiers

| Tier | Purpose |
|------|--------|
| Supporter | General support |
| Backer | Increased support |
| Founder | Early supporter |

---

### 2.3 Early Access / Lifetime

Optional:

- Lifetime Pro license (limited-time)
- Discounted pricing for early supporters

---

## 3. Transition to 1.0

At or after 1.0:

- Pro features become paid
- Business features become licensed
- Free remains fully usable

Users will receive advance notice.

---

## 4. Post-1.0 Pricing Model

### 4.1 Pro (Individual)

- Monthly subscription
- Yearly discount
- Optional lifetime license

---

### 4.2 Business (Organizations)

- Per-user pricing
- Team plans
- Enterprise agreements

---

## 5. Trust Principles

- No bait-and-switch
- Clear labeling of Pro features during beta
- Advance notice of pricing changes
- Free edition remains functional

---

## 6. Non-Goals

- Forced subscriptions for core functionality
- Locking users out of their systems
- Cloud dependency for basic features

---

## 7. Summary

Helm monetization is based on:

- Optional support during beta
- Paid upgrades after 1.0
- Strong free foundation
